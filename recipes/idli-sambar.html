Idli Sambar Recipe

Idli Sambar is a classic South Indian breakfast of soft, steamed rice-and-lentil cakes served with a tangy, vegetable-laden lentil stew. It is light, wholesome, and traditionally enjoyed with coconut chutney.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups idli batter (fermented rice and urad dal)
  • 1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables, chopped
  • 2 tbsp sambar powder
  • 1 tbsp tamarind pulp
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • A few curry leaves
  • Salt, to taste

Steps

  1. Pour the fermented batter into greased idli molds and steam for 10-12 minutes until fluffy.
  2. Boil the toor dal until soft, then mash it smooth.
  3. Cook the vegetables with tamarind pulp and sambar powder, then add the dal and simmer.
  4. Temper the mustard seeds and curry leaves in hot oil and stir into the sambar.
  5. Serve the hot idlis with the sambar and coconut chutney.
